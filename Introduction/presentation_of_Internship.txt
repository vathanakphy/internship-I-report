I. Introduction
This chapter introduces the internship by providing a brief overview of the internship program, its main objectives, and general information about the host company before moving into the technical details of the work.

1.1 Presentation of Internship
### 1.1 Presentation of Internship

An internship is an important part of the academic journey because it allows students to experience a real working environment before entering professional life. For Computer Science students, practical experience is important because theoretical knowledge alone is not enough to fully understand how software systems are developed, maintained, and used in real organizations.

I completed my internship at SINET, a technology and telecommunication company. During the internship, I was assigned as a Software Developer Intern in the Engineering & Operation Department. I worked with my supervisor, system engineers, and the Chief Technology Officer, which allowed me to understand both software development tasks and the company’s technical working environment.

My main responsibility was to enhance and extend the existing Attendance System, which had been operating for nearly two years across multiple branches of S.I Group. The work involved improving existing features, fixing issues, testing system behavior, preparing database changes, and supporting deployment preparation. Through this internship, I was able to participate in real software development activities and understand how an internal company system is improved based on actual business needs.

1.1.1 Objective of Internship

The main objective of this internship was to fulfill the academic requirement o at CADT and to gain practical working experience in a professional software development environment.

This internship help me apply the knowledge and skills learned during my studies to real software development tasks, especially in backend development, frontend development, database management, debugging, system testing, deployment and software documentation.

Another things was to understand how software systems are developed, improved, tested, deployed, and maintained in the industry. By working on the company’s Attendance System, I was able to learn how real business requirements are analyzed and transformed into system.

The internship also aimed to improve my professional skills, including communication, teamwork, responsibility, time management, problem-solving, and collaboration with supervisors and colleagues from different technical backgrounds.

1.1.2. Duration of Internship

The internship started on 20 April 2026 and ended on 12 July 2026, with a total duration of three months. During this period, I worked as a Software Developer Intern at SINET.

My working schedule was from Monday to Friday, from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, and on Saturday morning from 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM. Sunday was the weekly rest day.
