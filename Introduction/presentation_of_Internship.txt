I. Introduction

1.1 Presentation of Internship

An internship is an important part of the academic journey because it allows students to apply the theoretical knowledge gained from university to a real working environment. For students, an internship provides valuable practical experience before entering the professional workplace. It helps students understand how academic concepts are used in real situations and how professional organizations operate.

An internship is not only about working in a company, but also about learning from senior staff, adapting to the workplace, understanding company workflows, and building professional relationships with colleagues. These experiences are important because they help students develop both technical and soft skills. As a Computer Science student, theoretical knowledge alone is not sufficient to demonstrate professional ability. Practical experience is also necessary to evaluate whether a student can apply knowledge effectively in real software development environments.

During my internship at SINET, I had the opportunity to develop my potential not only in software development but also in understanding other areas related to the technology industry. SINET is a technology and telecommunication company with different departments and technical fields. Working in this environment allowed me to communicate with colleagues from different backgrounds and gain a broader understanding of how technology supports company operations.

During the internship, I was assigned to work as a Software Developer Intern in the Engineering & Operation Department. I worked collaboratively with my supervisor, who is a software developer, as well as with system engineers and the Chief Technology Officer. Through this collaboration, I gained knowledge not only in software engineering, but also in management, teamwork, and professional communication.

My main responsibility was to enhance and extend the existing Attendance System, which has been operating for nearly two years across multiple branches of S.I Group. My work was not limited to writing code. I also followed the software development workflow, which included understanding requirements, analyzing problems, improving existing features, fixing bugs, testing, preparing database changes, and supporting deployment preparation. I worked with frontend development, backend development, database operations, and deployment-related tasks.

Throughout the three-month internship, I gained valuable experience as a Computer Science student. I improved my technical skills and gained a better understanding of how software systems are developed, tested, deployed, and maintained according to industry practices. I also learned the importance of system reliability, data safety, and risk reduction when working with production-level systems. In addition to technical knowledge, I improved my communication and problem-solving skills by working with colleagues who had different roles and responsibilities.

Overall, this internship helped me connect academic knowledge with real industry practice. It allowed me to leave my comfort zone, explore a professional working environment, and understand the responsibilities of a software developer in a real company. The experience gained from this internship will support my future career development and strengthen my preparation for working in the software engineering field after graduation.
