The project planning was based on business priority and user requirements. Since the Attendance System was already operating in the real company environment, the work was not planned as a fixed project with strict deadlines. Instead, tasks were assigned step by step based on priority, system issues, and requests from the main users of the system.

Most tasks were requested or reviewed by the CTO, HR staff, and team leaders, who were directly involved in using or managing the Attendance System. The development process focused on completing one task or feature first, testing it, fixing issues, and then preparing it for release before moving to the next task. This approach helped reduce pressure and lower the risk of affecting the production system.

The project work was divided into several phases, starting from system optimization, QR enhancement, feature improvement, company structure improvement, leave management, report analysis, quality assurance, and maintenance. Each phase was handled based on business impact, approval, and system readiness.

The development lifecycle followed during the project can be summarized as follows:

Analyze the requirement or problem reported by users.
Discuss and confirm the expected result with the CTO, HR staff, or team leaders.
Implement the feature or improvement in the development environment.
Test the feature and fix bugs found during testing.
Prepare the feature for pre-production or production release.
Allow the CTO to review the work.
Coordinate with the system engineer for deployment.
Monitor the result after release and apply hotfixes if needed.

Since the system was related to real attendance data and payroll processing, changes had to be released carefully. Small and controlled changes were preferred because they were easier to test, review, and rollback if problems occurred.

Overall, the planning of the project followed an iterative development approach. The work was divided into small phases so that each improvement could be analyzed, developed, tested, reviewed, and released with lower risk. This approach was suitable because the system was already used in daily operations and any issue could affect attendance tracking, HR work, and payroll preparation.
